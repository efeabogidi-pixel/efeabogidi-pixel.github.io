Mapping Extreme Urban Heat Vulnerability — Phoenix, AZ

A GIS case study combining environmental exposure, neighborhood vulnerability, and access to cooling resources.

← Back to Projects View StoryMap

Overview

This project maps neighborhood-level vulnerability to extreme heat in Phoenix by integrating land surface temperature (LST), vegetation (NDVI), demographic vulnerability, and access to cooling resources. The goal is to create clear outputs that help identify where risk may be concentrated.

Why it matters

Heat risk is not evenly distributed. Neighborhood conditions, built environment, and access to resources can amplify exposure — especially for vulnerable populations.

Data

  • Census tracts for the Phoenix area
  • Land surface temperature raster (heat exposure)
  • NDVI / vegetation raster (heat mitigation)
  • Demographic indicators (vulnerability context)
  • Cooling centers (access/proximity)

Method

  • Prepared and clipped datasets to the study area
  • Summarized raster variables to tracts (zonal statistics)
  • Calculated access indicators for cooling centers (proximity/service logic)
  • Normalized variables to a common scale
  • Combined indicators into a composite vulnerability index
  • Mapped results using consistent classification and clean layouts

Outputs

  • Heat exposure (LST) map
  • Vegetation (NDVI) map
  • Cooling access indicator
  • Final vulnerability index map
  • StoryMap narrative connecting results to context

Limitations & Next Steps

  • Strengthen temporal alignment between raster layers and demographic data
  • Test different weighting strategies and sensitivity checks
  • Add tree canopy, impervious surface, shade, and heat-health outcomes
  • Validate with local knowledge and ground-level context

Tools

ArcGIS Pro • Raster tools • Zonal statistics • Joins/field calculations • Professional cartography • ArcGIS StoryMaps
