Urban Heat Vulnerability — Phoenix, AZ

A neighborhood-level analysis of extreme heat exposure and vulnerability using environmental + demographic indicators.

Key Questions

• Which neighborhoods experience the highest heat exposure?

• How do vegetation and land cover influence surface temperature?

• Which populations are most vulnerable to extreme heat?

Methods (high level)

• Derived land surface temperature (LST) and vegetation (NDVI)

• Joined demographic indicators at neighborhood/tract level

• Compared spatial patterns to identify high-risk areas

Outcome

The results highlight spatial inequities in heat exposure and provide insight for urban planning, public health, and climate resilience strategies.
