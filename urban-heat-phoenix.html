Phoenix Urban Heat Vulnerability Index

Case study: combining environmental exposure, neighborhood vulnerability, and access to cooling resources.

← Back Contact

Overview

I created a tract-level heat vulnerability index for Phoenix by integrating environmental indicators (e.g., land surface temperature and vegetation cover) with socioeconomic and accessibility variables.

Problem

Extreme heat is not evenly experienced across a city. Neighborhood conditions, resources, and access can amplify risk — especially for vulnerable populations.

Data

  • Census tracts (Phoenix/Maricopa area)
  • Land surface temperature (raster)
  • NDVI / vegetation cover (raster)
  • Demographic indicators (tabular)
  • Cooling centers (points)

Method

  • Standardized projection and clipped all layers to the study area
  • Extracted raster summaries to tracts (zonal statistics)
  • Calculated access/proximity to cooling centers
  • Normalized variables and built a composite index
  • Mapped results using consistent classification and layouts

Results

  • Highlighted neighborhoods with high heat exposure and low vegetation
  • Identified areas where vulnerable demographics overlap with poor access to cooling
  • Created a tract-level index to support prioritization and communication

Deliverables

  • Heat exposure maps (LST)
  • Vegetation maps (NDVI)
  • Accessibility to cooling centers
  • Final vulnerability index map + documentation

Limitations & Next Steps

  • Improve temporal matching across datasets (season/time-of-day)
  • Test alternative weighting strategies and sensitivity analysis
  • Add shade, tree canopy, and impervious surface variables

Tools

ArcGIS Pro • Raster tools • Zonal Statistics • Network/Proximity analysis • Clean map layout standards
