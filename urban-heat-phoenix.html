Urban Heat Vulnerability — Phoenix, AZ

This project analyzes neighborhood-level vulnerability to extreme heat in Phoenix, Arizona. It integrates environmental indicators and demographic context to identify communities most at risk and support climate resilience planning.

Key Questions

  • Which neighborhoods experience the highest heat exposure?
  • How do vegetation and land cover influence surface temperature?
  • Which populations are most vulnerable to extreme heat?

Data & Methods

  • Land Surface Temperature (Landsat)
  • NDVI (Vegetation Index)
  • U.S. Census demographic data
  • Spatial joins and zonal statistics in ArcGIS Pro

Outcome

Results highlight spatial inequities in heat exposure and provide insight for urban planning, public health, and climate resilience strategies.
