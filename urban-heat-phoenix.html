Case Study

Urban Heat Vulnerability — Phoenix, AZ

This project analyzes neighborhood-level vulnerability to extreme heat in Phoenix, Arizona. It combines environmental conditions (LST, vegetation), socioeconomic indicators, and accessibility concepts to identify communities most at risk.

Key Questions

Where is heat exposure highest? How does NDVI relate to temperature? Which communities face the most risk?

Data & Methods

Core layers typically include land surface temperature, vegetation index (NDVI), and demographic vulnerability indicators. The workflow uses GIS joins, zonal statistics, and map design in ArcGIS Pro to summarize patterns at neighborhood/tract scales.
