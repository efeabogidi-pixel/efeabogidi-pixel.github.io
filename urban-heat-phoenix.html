Urban Heat Vulnerability – Phoenix, AZ

This project analyzes neighborhood-level vulnerability to extreme heat in Phoenix, Arizona. It integrates environmental, demographic, and spatial accessibility data to identify communities most at risk.

Key Questions

  • Which neighborhoods experience the highest heat exposure?
  • How does vegetation and land cover influence surface temperature?
  • Which populations are most vulnerable to extreme heat?

Data & Methods

  • Land Surface Temperature (Landsat)
  • NDVI (Vegetation Index)
  • U.S. Census demographic data
  • Spatial joins and zonal statistics in ArcGIS Pro

Outcome

The results highlight spatial inequities in heat exposure and provide insight for urban planning, public health, and climate resilience strategies.
